Little Scout · UX flow review & scaffold · wireframe
From login → discover → updates on your spots
Low-fi flows for families with young kids. Three distinct navigation models, each carrying the same job-to-be-done: get parents signed in fast, help them find kid-friendly spots, and give them a reason to come back — updates from restaurants they've saved.
What works today
✓  Discovery is map + list with TTF tier dots — browsing is public, sign-in only gates contributing.
✓  Restaurant detail leads with the flagship kid food speed number + parent attributes.
✓  Web & iOS share the same vocabulary (tiers, notes, Apple sign-in).
Gaps this scaffold fixes
✗  No Saved / favorites — nowhere to collect spots you trust.
✗  No updates feed — new TTF data & notes on saved spots are invisible.
✗  Onboarding skips kids' ages, so nothing is personalized.
✗  Home is a one-time chooser, not a destination worth reopening.

Part 2 · How watchlist updates surface
Little Scout · Watchlist updates · how they surface
"A new review landed" — without being a pest
One principle drives every screen below: default to ambient, earn the interruption. A new visit logged on a saved spot is quiet by default; only the changes a parent explicitly opted into — and only when bundled and outside quiet hours — ever earn a push.
The loudness ladder
Quietest on the left. Most updates never climb past step 2.
1 · Ambient
always on · 0 interruption
A dot on the Saved tab, a row marker, a tab-title count. Nothing pops. The app just quietly carries the news until you look.
2 · Inbox (pull)
on demand · you go to it
Tap the bell / banner to open the full activity list, grouped by spot. You choose the moment — it never chooses for you.
3 · In-app toast
only while app is open
A slim, auto-dismissing bar when something relevant lands while you're already looking. Never stacks, never blocks a tap.
4 · Push (out of app)
opt-in · bundled · quiet-hours
Only events you switched on, bundled into one message, suppressed 8pm–8am, capped at 1/spot/day. Off by default.
⛔ Bundle — one summary, never N pings 🌙 Quiet hours 8pm–8am (family default) ⏱ Max 1 push / spot / day 🤝 Prime in context, never on launch

Part 4 · Web experience audit — Explore + Restaurant detail
Little Scout · Web experience audit · Explore + Restaurant detail
Fewer boxes, clearer hierarchy
Read from your real ExploreMapPage, MapSearchSidebar, and RestaurantDetailPage. The two pages share one disease: everything is an equally-weighted bordered box, stacked vertically. Below: what's there now, the specific problems, and a calmer redesign for each.
Explore — what's slowing it down
4 stacked filter rows. Town, ZIP, Type, and Quality each render as their own horizontally-scrolling chip nav with a bottom border — a wall of controls before a single result.
Heavy result cards. Every row is a bordered + shadowed card carrying up to 3 badges (Speed · ★ · 💬). Dozens of boxes-in-boxes.
The map loses. A tall, busy 24rem rail squeezes the thing people actually came for.
No saved / updates entry point anywhere on the surface parents use most.
Detail — what's slowing it down
6 near-identical cards. Hours, Share-your-visit, Community activity, Kid food speed, Parent ratings, Notes — all the same titled box, same weight, one on top of another.
The flagship is buried. "Kid food speed" — the whole reason for the app — is the 5th card down.
A CTA before any data. "Share your visit" sits above the speed and ratings it's asking you to trust.
No hierarchy. A phone number, a chart, and the headline metric all look equally important.

Part 5 · Explore filters — make geography dynamic
Little Scout · main branch · ExploreFilterBar · exploreFacets.ts
Let the map decide the area
First, a correction from reading main: the Town / ZIP / cuisine lists aren't hard-coded — buildExploreFacets() already generates them at runtime by counting addresses. The real problem is which set it counts, and that the dropdown and the map don't talk to each other. Below: what's actually happening, then three ways to make the area follow the map.
What main actually does
Facets are derived, not fixed. buildExploreFacets() runs countBy() over the loaded list to build the town dropdown, ZIP chips, and cuisine chips with live counts.
Geography is parsed from a string. City = the 3rd comma-segment of the address; ZIP = a regex. No place IDs, no coordinates feeding the filter.
The map already loads by viewport. Panning fires a debounced bbox fetch — the data is location-aware; the filter UI just isn't.
Why it still feels static
Chips count the whole cache, not the view. Home preloads the full catalog into the same cache, so "Dedham (12)" lingers after you've panned to Needham. Chips show everywhere you've loaded, not here.
Address parsing is brittle. Any non-standard address drops out of the town list silently.
Two disconnected controls. Picking a town filters the list but never moves the map; panning the map never updates the active town. The control duplicates — and drifts from — what the map already shows.
