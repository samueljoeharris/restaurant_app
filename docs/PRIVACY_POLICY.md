# Little Scout — Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** 17 June 2026

Little Scout ("Little Scout", "we", "us") helps parents share and discover how
quickly kid-friendly restaurants serve food. This policy explains what we
collect, why, how long we keep it, who we share it with, and the choices you
have. It applies to the Little Scout web app and iOS app.

If you have questions about this policy, contact us at **privacy@littlescout.app**.

> Note: `privacy@littlescout.app` is the intended contact address and must be
> provisioned (or replaced with a confirmed address) before App Store
> submission.

## Who this app is for

Little Scout is intended for adults (typically parents and caregivers). It is
**not directed to children under 13**, and we do not knowingly collect personal
information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us
personal information, contact us and we will delete it.

## What we collect

| Data | Why we collect it |
|------|-------------------|
| Email address | To create and secure your account (authentication) |
| Ratings and observations you submit (e.g. time-to-food, restaurant notes) | To operate the crowd-sourced rating product and compute aggregates |
| Approximate location — **only when you explicitly grant it** | To center the map near you and find restaurants nearby |
| Technical/abuse-prevention data (IP address, device/request metadata in server logs) | Security, rate limiting, and reliability |

We do **not** collect a continuous or background location trail. Location is
used only in the moment, when you tap to find restaurants near you, and is not
stored as a personal location history.

## How we use your information

- Operate and improve the rating aggregation product.
- Authenticate you and keep your account secure.
- Prevent abuse, spam, and fraudulent submissions.
- Compute and display aggregate restaurant statistics. Aggregates are derived
  from many users' contributions and are not attributed to you individually in
  the public app.

We do **not** sell your personal information, and we do not use third-party
advertising or cross-app tracking SDKs.

## How long we keep it

| Data | Retention |
|------|-----------|
| Account email | Until you delete your account |
| Ratings, observations, and notes you submit | Until you delete your account (then deleted or anonymized — see "Your rights") |
| Curated restaurant locations (our own catalog data) | Indefinitely |
| Server logs containing IP/device data | 30–90 days, then aggregated or redacted |

## Who we share it with (service providers)

We use the following third parties to run Little Scout. They process data on our
behalf, subject to their own terms and privacy policies:

- **Firebase (Google)** — account authentication and security.
- **Google Maps Platform** — restaurant location, map display, and search.
- **Google Cloud Platform** — hosting and storage (Cloud Run, Cloud SQL, and
  Cloud Storage for any photo uploads).

## Your rights and choices

- **Access and deletion:** Delete your account from **Account → Delete account** in the web or iOS app. You may need to confirm your identity before deletion completes. When you delete your account, we delete your ratings, observations, and notes, delete any photos you uploaded, revoke your sign-in credentials (including Sign in with Apple), and remove your authentication record, subject to legal retention requirements. Deletion is usually immediate; we show a confirmation when it is done.
- **Help deleting:** If you cannot access the app, email **privacy@littlescout.app**.
- **Location:** You can decline or revoke the location permission at any time in
  your browser or device settings; the app falls back to a default map area.
- **Regional rights (CCPA / GDPR):** Depending on where you live, you may have
  rights to access, correct, or delete your personal information, or to object to
  certain processing. We honor these requests through the same contact above.

## Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the product evolves. We will update the "Last
updated" date above and, for material changes, provide a more prominent notice.
